Marine Mobility & Boat Sharing System

End-to-End Hardware & HMI Architecture

Technical Delivery Completed Awaiting Client Certification

Designed and engineered the complete hardware, firmware, and user interface for a commercial "Boat Sharing" startup aimed at lakes in Poland and Switzerland. The project involved creating two distinct electronic architectures for two types of vessels: a 5m single-engine utility boat and a 7m twin-engine recreational catamaran.

Arduino Mega 2560 BLDC Motor Control (1000W) Nextion 7" HMI C++ Firmware RFID Edge-Billing Differential Steering (Tank-style) Custom Battery BMS Logic I2C / 1-Wire (DS18B20)

Architectural Highlight: "Offline" Edge-Billing

One of the biggest challenges in marine environments is unreliable network coverage. Instead of building a complex, network-dependent cloud infrastructure, I designed an edge-computing billing logic based on RFID technology.

  • The user must keep their assigned RFID card in the slot to unlock the engine (immobilizer relay).
  • Firmware calculates the elapsed usage time (to the minute) and writes this data directly to the RFID card's memory continuously.
  • Upon returning to the port, the reception software simply reads the card, calculates the fee based on the stored minutes, and wipes the card for the next user. A highly resilient, 100% offline solution.

Fleet Technical Specifications

Utility / Fishing Boat (5m)

Designed for reliability and utility, featuring a single-engine propulsion system.

  • Propulsion: 1x 1000W Golden Motor BLDC (48V) with classic tiller handle and Hall sensor throttle.
  • Display: Industrial 320x128 LCD screen showing RTC time, accumulated trip time, and battery range estimation.
  • Telemetry: 2x DS18B20 digital sensors for highly accurate (0.1°C) ambient and water temperature readings.
  • Safety: Custom battery voltage monitoring circuit to prevent deep discharge, dynamically calculating "remaining sailing hours".

Recreational Catamaran (7m+)

A flagship vessel requiring advanced HMI and complex motor coordination.

  • Propulsion: Twin 1000W BLDC Motors.
  • Control Interface: Custom 3-axis joystick (pitch, roll, yaw/twist with auto-return).
  • Differential Steering: Programmed "tank-style" steering, allowing the catamaran to perform zero-radius turns in tight marinas (one engine full forward, one full reverse).
  • HMI: 7-inch Nextion Touchscreen displaying full telemetry, trip data, and controlling deck LED lighting systems.
