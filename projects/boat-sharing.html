Project Context & The Challenge

The client aimed to create a self-service, automated boat rental system—similar to a car-sharing model—designed for lakes in Poland and Switzerland. The project required engineering the complete electronic architecture from scratch for two distinct vessels: a rugged 5-meter single-engine utility boat, and a luxurious 7-meter recreational catamaran.

I was brought in to take ownership of the hardware integration, motor control, and HMI. The biggest challenge was designing a highly reliable, End-to-End (E2E) billing solution that could operate flawlessly on open water, where GSM or WiFi connectivity is notoriously unreliable.

System Architecture & "Offline" Edge-Billing

Instead of relying on a central server that could act as a single point of failure on the lake, I designed a resilient edge-computing architecture based on RFID technology. The user's RFID card acts as both the physical key and the database.

Edge-Billing Logic

An authorized RFID card must remain in the slot to unlock the motor relays. The microcontroller calculates elapsed usage time (down to the minute) and continuously writes this data directly to the RFID card's memory. When the user returns to the port, the reception software simply reads the card to calculate the fee. No internet required.

Battery & Telemetry Management

Built custom logic to monitor the 48V battery pack, preventing deep discharge by translating voltage into a user-friendly "remaining sailing hours" metric. Digital DS18B20 sensors provide precise (0.1°C) ambient and water temperature readings directly to the HMI.

Fleet Technical Specifications

The system required two distinct hardware and UX approaches based on the vessel's purpose. Both systems share the same core billing logic (Arduino Mega 2560) but differ drastically in motor control and user interface.

Utility Boat (5m)

Designed for reliability and fishing trips.

  • Propulsion: Single 1000W BLDC motor with classic tiller handle and Hall sensor throttle.
  • Interface: Industrial 320x128 LCD screen (non-touch) displaying RTC time, elapsed trip time, and telemetry.
  • Safety: Relay-based engine immobilizer tied directly to the RFID presence.

Catamaran (7m+)

A recreational flagship requiring complex coordination.

  • Propulsion: Twin 1000W BLDC motors.
  • Control: Custom 3-axis joystick (pitch, roll, twist) enabling "tank-style" differential steering (zero-radius turns).
  • Interface: Advanced 7-inch Nextion Touchscreen displaying telemetry and controlling deck LED lighting.
