Project Objectives & Scope

The objective was to engineer a robust, commercial-grade automated rental ecosystem for electric vessels, deployed across inland waterways in Poland and Switzerland. The project scope encompassed the design and implementation of a proprietary electronic architecture for two distinct vessel archetypes: a rugged 5-meter utility unit and a premium 7-meter recreational catamaran.

As the Lead System Architect, I assumed full responsibility for hardware selection, PCB design, firmware development, and propulsion logic integration. The critical requirement was to deliver a fault-tolerant, decentralized billing infrastructure capable of operating reliably in connectivity-denied environments common on open lakes.

System Architecture & Edge Computing

To eliminate Single Points of Failure (SPOF) associated with cloud-dependent systems, a resilient edge-computing model was implemented. The system utilizes the user's RFID credential as a secure, portable database.

Distributed Transaction Logic

The embedded controller enforces a hardware-level interlock. Propulsion relays are energized only upon validation of an encrypted RFID token. Usage telemetry is calculated in real-time and written directly to the card's EEPROM, enabling offline settlement at the marina reception.

Battery Management Integration

The ECU interfaces with the 48V LiFePO4 bank, translating raw voltage data into a "Remaining Time" metric for the end-user. Safety algorithms include "Limp Mode" activation to ensure return-to-base capability during low-charge events.

Fleet Technical Specifications

The architecture is modular, allowing the core billing logic to be reused across different propulsion topologies.

Utility Class (5m)

Propulsion: Single 1000W BLDC (Tiller/Hall Sensor).

HMI: Industrial monochrome LCD (320x240) for high-contrast readability.

Safety: Hardware-based immobilization circuit tied to RFID presence.

Catamaran Class (7m+)

Propulsion: Twin 1000W BLDC configuration.

Control: Differential Thrust Algorithm (Tank Steering) via 3-axis industrial joystick.

HMI: 7-inch Nextion Touchscreen handling telemetry and auxiliary systems (lighting/audio).

Production & Assembly

Final Assembly Phase: Helm station integration (Catamaran Class).
Structural Fit-out: Harness routing and mounting preparation (Utility Class).

Engineering Implementation: Hardware & HMI

The system relies on a custom-designed ECU utilizing an ATmega2560 core, featuring optically isolated I/O to mitigate noise from the high-current traction system.

Propulsion Logic & Field Validation

The twin-engine catamaran required a complex "Drive-by-Wire" strategy. I engineered a differential thrust algorithm that translates X/Y cartesian inputs from the joystick into independent PWM signals for port and starboard motors, enabling zero-radius maneuverability.

Technical delivery has been successfully executed. Both control architectures were engineered, programmed, and validated in operational environments. The project demonstrates the capability to deliver production-ready, resilient embedded systems for commercial maritime applications.
