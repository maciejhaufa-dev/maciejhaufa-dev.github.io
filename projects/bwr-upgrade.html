Deep Modernization of BWR-1S & BWR-1D Vehicles

Leading the electrical system integration for the deep modernization of Soviet-era reconnaissance vehicles, transforming them into state-of-the-art platforms compliant with modern NATO battlefield standards.

Note: Due to the confidential nature of this defense project, this overview is based on publicly available information. Specific technical implementation details remain classified.

The Modernization Goal

The primary objective was to extend the operational life and significantly enhance the capabilities of the BWR-1S and BWR-1D reconnaissance vehicles. This involved replacing obsolete Soviet-era systems with modern, NATO-compliant technology, including advanced observation, communication, and battlefield management systems. My role was central to ensuring these new systems could be seamlessly integrated into the existing vehicle architecture.

My Role: Architect of the New Electrical Backbone

As the Lead Electrical & Systems Integration Engineer, I was responsible for the complete redesign and implementation of the vehicle's electrical infrastructure. This included:

  • **Defining the new electrical system architecture** to support high-power, modern C4I systems.
  • **Designing and overseeing the manufacturing of new wire harnesses** compliant with military standards.
  • **Managing the integration** of dozens of new subsystems, including thermal cameras, laser rangefinders, BMS, and digital communication platforms.
  • **Leading the system validation process** during factory acceptance tests (FAT) and live polygon trials, including amphibious testing.
  • **Mentoring a team of engineers** and ensuring all technical documentation met stringent AQAP and military requirements.
