Commercial Product Project Manager & Lead R&D Engineer

Viperwing Combat Simulators

Leading the product evolution from software-based interfaces to high-fidelity, 1:1 scale physical cockpits, delivering an unparalleled level of realism and tactile immersion for professional pilot training.

The Challenge: Bridging the Gap Between Simulation and Reality

The initial versions of the simulators relied on touchscreen panels to replicate the complex instrumentation of F-16 and F-35 cockpits. While functional, this approach lacked the critical **tactile feedback** and muscle memory development essential for effective pilot training. The primary goal was to transition to a fully physical, 1:1 scale cockpit to dramatically increase training realism and immersion.

My Role: Architect of the Physical Cockpit

As Project Manager and Lead R&D Engineer, I was responsible for the entire hardware integration lifecycle. This involved:

  • **Conceptual Design:** Defining the architecture for a modular, hardware-driven cockpit.
  • **Hardware-Software Integration:** Designing and implementing custom embedded systems to interface with the simulation software.
  • **Prototyping & Manufacturing:** Overseeing the creation of physical panels, switches, and gauges.
  • **System Validation:** Ensuring that every physical control responded with zero-latency and high fidelity.

The Solution: A Distributed USB/HID Interface

To manage the complexity of hundreds of individual controls (toggle switches, rotary encoders, potentiometers, LED indicators), I designed a **distributed embedded system**. Each cockpit panel was equipped with its own microcontroller, which communicated with a central hub. This hub aggregated all inputs and presented them to the main simulation computer as a standard **USB Human Interface Device (HID)**.

This architecture ensured:

  • **Modularity and Scalability:** Easy to add or replace cockpit panels without redesigning the entire system.
  • **Zero-Latency Response:** Critical for realistic flight simulation.
  • **Plug-and-Play Compatibility:** The system was recognized by the simulation software without requiring custom drivers.

The Result: Unprecedented Realism

The successful transition to physical cockpits transformed the Viperwing simulators into top-tier training tools. The introduction of realistic tactile feedback significantly enhanced muscle memory development and procedural training for pilots, solidifying the product's position as a leader in the high-fidelity simulation market.

The final product is a testament to the power of integrating robust embedded engineering with advanced simulation software.
