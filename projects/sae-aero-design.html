The Challenge: Engineering an Award-Winning UAV

The SAE Aero Design competition is a prestigious international challenge where teams design, build, and validate a custom, high-performance UAV. This is not just an academic exercise; it's a rigorous engineering process demanding months of intensive R&D, prototyping, and testing to meet extreme performance and weight constraints. Our team was committed to pushing the boundaries of what was possible.

My Role: Team Captain & Lead Integrator

As the official Team Captain, my responsibilities went beyond pure engineering. I was the central hub for the project, ensuring that our design was not only technically sound but also strategically aligned with the competition's objectives.

  • Leadership & Coordination: Managed the workflow between sub-teams (aerodynamics, structures, electronics) to ensure a cohesive and integrated final design.
  • Technical Authority: Made final decisions on critical design trade-offs to balance performance, weight, and reliability.
  • Lead Author of Technical Documentation: Personally authored the comprehensive technical report that was judged 1st in the world at the SAE Aero Design West competition and later became my Engineering Thesis.
  • Prototyping & Manufacturing: Led hands-on fabrication, from composite material layup to final assembly, ensuring every component met our exacting design specifications.
  • Technical Documentation & Thesis: As the lead author of the technical report, I documented our entire design process. This report not only won 1st place in its category but also formed the basis of my successful Engineer's Thesis defense, as mentioned on the official team website.
