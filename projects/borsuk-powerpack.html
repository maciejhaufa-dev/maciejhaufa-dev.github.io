BWP "Borsuk" PowerPack Development & Diagnostics

Co-developing the powertrain unit for Poland's new Infantry Fighting Vehicle (BWP Borsuk) and architecting a custom diagnostic system to streamline production, testing, and field maintenance.

Note: Due to the confidential nature of this defense project, this overview is based on publicly available information. Specific technical implementation details remain classified.

The Project Scope: A New Heart for a New IFV

The BWP "Borsuk" is a new generation amphibious Infantry Fighting Vehicle, and its PowerPack is the core of its mobility. My involvement covered the entire lifecycle, from the R&D of prototypes to supporting the serial production line. The main challenge was to ensure the highest level of reliability and performance for a system that must operate in extreme conditions.

My Role: From R&D to Production Line Optimization

As a Lead R&D Engineer, my responsibilities were twofold: contributing to the core development of the PowerPack and solving real-world production challenges.

  1. PowerPack Co-Development: I was actively involved in designing, building, and testing the prototypes and serial models of the drive unit, working in close cooperation with HSW S.A.
  2. Diagnostic System Architect: To address production bottlenecks and improve field serviceability, I designed and built a ruggedized, portable diagnostic station. This "Suitcase Tester" allows for:
    • Full control of the engine and transmission outside the vehicle.
    • Real-time CAN bus logging and analysis.
    • Simulation of critical faults for training and QA purposes.
  3. Field Calibration & Validation: I conducted hands-on calibration of the drive system during polygon trials, optimizing the engine and transmission parameters to achieve the maximum 530kN tractive potential.
