Project Context & Closed-Loop Logic

To maximize the efficiency of photovoltaic (PV) panels, they must remain perpendicular to the sun's rays throughout the day. I was tasked with designing an autonomous control unit capable of driving a heavy-duty, dual-axis mechanical structure.

Instead of relying on complex astronomical algorithms, I designed a reactive, closed-loop differential system. I engineered a custom cross-configured Light Dependent Resistor (LDR) array. The microcontroller continuously evaluates the analog voltage differentials across the 4 quadrants of the sensor, triggering the X and Y-axis motor relays until a state of luminous equilibrium is reached.

Edge-Cases & Structural Fail-Safes

A mature industrial system must account for environmental hazards and mechanical boundaries. The core tracking logic is overridden by critical operational states:

Storm Protection (Anemometer)

High winds pose a severe structural threat to large PV arrays. The system continuously polls an anemometer. If wind speeds exceed a critical threshold (e.g., 20 m/s), the MCU triggers a hardware override, driving the motors to park the panels in a flat, horizontal orientation to minimize aerodynamic drag.

Dawn Anticipation & Limit Switches

After sunset, the system automatically executes a homing sequence, returning the azimuthal axis to the East to capture early morning rays. This sequence relies on hardware limit switches to safely establish the absolute zero-position, preventing mechanical over-travel and structural damage.

Evolution to v2.0: Introducing the HMI

Version 1.0 of the system was hardcoded. To make the system commercially viable and user-friendly, I developed Version 2.0.

V2 integrated a localized Human-Machine Interface (HMI) featuring a 2x16 LCD and a 4-button tactile keypad (Back, +, -, Enter). This empowered the end-user to dynamically adjust critical parameters such as LDR sensitivity margins, wind speed thresholds, and motor actuation delays. All user-defined parameters are securely stored in the MCU's EEPROM.
